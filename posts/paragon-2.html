Paragon 2?

Aug 23th 2022 4 Min read

Paragon was a MOBA released in 2016 by Epic Games, it was a breathe of fresh air for MOBA games due to it's third person camera angle and unlike other MOBA's you could freely aim when in combat, unfortunately the game closed down 2 years later in 2018, this was due to Epic putting sole focus into Fortnite their most successful and profitable game to date, this and that Paragon didnt meet growth expectations.

There is a silver lining though, Epic released all the game assets for free on Unreal Engine, Paragon had a loyal fan base and a few companies have taken the opportunity to revive the game.

Their are currently 3 games that have used the assets to create a new Paragon:


  • ◉ Fault: Elder Orb (Released)
  • ◉ Predecessor (Due in early access at the end of the 2022
  • ◉ Overprime (Expected to release in 2023)

Fault Elder Orb released in 2020, this was the first company to attempt to revive Paragon, it didn't go to plan though as fans of the original were dissappointed with the games bugs, balance and general care put into making it, Fault has recently become free-to-play to try and bring some players in but ultimately didn't work.


Predecessor, this is the one to watch out for, expected to be in early access by the end of the year (2022), Omega Studios (The Developers) announced they had raised $2.2 million to create a cross platfrom, third-person MOBA. Which utilizes the assets from Paragon, first look at this one looks alot cleaner and smoother than Fault: Elder Prime, and is certainly in the winnings of the 3 games trying to achieve what Paragon created.
