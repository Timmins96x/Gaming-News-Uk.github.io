For Honor Review (2022)

Sep 15th 2022 5 Min read

For Honor released in 2017, it was one of the most hyped games in 2016 to release due to it's unique combat mechanics and graphics. The game was a hit and miss on launch due to the servers having bad connection issues which was highly frustrating for a game that relies on quick responses as you battle other players.

But where is the game now?

Read on for changes and improvements below:



For Honor is currently on Year 6 Season 2 at the moment, each season has seen many improvements such as bug fixes, new maps, new modes, battle pass and even a new character.


The game is still very much alive with the latest hero being released at the end of July,"The Medjay", an Egyptian fighter who wields a fan axe, the overall hero roster has massively improved since laungh, with all sorts of warriors, from Ninja's to pirates, there's plenty of variety to choose from.

A big milestone for the game was the release of the Marching Fire dlc in 2018, it brought four new hero's a completely new faction "Wu Lin", new maps and a personal favourite a new game mode called Breach, which is a siege mode where one team is defending a castle while the other is trying to conquer it.


Overall:

The game has had thousands of updates mainly balance changes, but they have fixed all the major issues by making it a peer-to-peer connection instead of running through the games servers, They've added plenty of new and exciting hero's, game modes, cosmetics and maps.

For Honor is definitely worth trying again it's a game i hope will never fade away due to it's intense combat system.

P.S. Ubisoft if you're reading this add lightsabers!
